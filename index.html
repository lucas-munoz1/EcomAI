Understanding the Product Analysis Engine

The e-commerce product analysis engine is a culmination of data scraping, machine learning models, and natural language processing techniques that are used to analyze e-commerce product’s customer reviews.

The analysis engine has two primary functions:

1. To find and exploit market gaps across a product's market
2. To extract high quality product intelligence from customer reviews

The following sections describe the key components of the engine, and how they relate to the engine's analysis capabilities.

Text Classification

The text classification model is a vital component of the analysis engine which uses a pre-trained deep learning model for contextual word embeddings to detect the presence of market gap language within a customer review.

Sentiment Analysis

The sentiment analysis model, a key component of the analysis engine, classifies customer review subtitles as positive, neutral, or negative. This model enriches our analytical capabilities by enabling us to isolate reviews by sentiment, and therefore shift our focus between positive and negative product attributes as needed.

The final component of the engine is a collection of natural language processing methods, feature extraction techniques, topic modeling algorithms and visualization tools, which allows us to process and interpret large groups of customer reviews.

Identifying Profitable Product Characteristics in the Fishing Apparel Market Using Market Gap Analysis

The Opportunity

‘The United States is currently experiencing a surge in demand for high-quality fishing equipment, driven by a growing interest in recreational fishing among its population.’

In 2024, retailers in the fishing equipment industry face a rapidly expanding market. During the Covid-19 pandemic, online sales of fishing tackle products – a subcategory of fishing equipment - grew by 40%. This surge contributed to fishing equipment revenues reaching $1.6 billion in the United States in 2024, a 6% increase from 2023.

To find a product capable of capitalizing on this growth, the product analysis engine was used to analyze thousands of customer reviews for fishing equipment products, and pinpoint a set of specific, solvable market gaps.

The Solution

The primary goal of this analysis was to find a product with a market gap opportunity. To accomplish this, we took advantage of the text classification model in the product analysis engine, which specializes in extracting market gap language from customer reviews.

Broad Market Gap Analysis

First, a broad market gap analysis was employed to find fishing equipment products where large amounts of customer reviews had market gap language. Using the analysis engine to scrape product data, we collected and compared data for products such as fishing gloves, lures, and vests.

The product comparisons were made based on statistics generated from the sentiment analysis and text classification models. The primary metrics generated included the average number of positive, negative, and neutral customer reviews for each product category, as well as a 'market gap score,' which represents the percentage of customer reviews with market gap language.

Our first observation was that fishing vests had a significantly higher than average market gap score, at 16.6%. Furthermore, an above-average number of neutral reviews indicated product adequacy, but room for improvement. Based on these two factors, fishing vests stood out as the leading contender for a product with a market gap opportunity.

Targeted Market Gap Analysis

The second component of this process was a targeted market gap analysis for fishing vest products. By repeating the previous process, but for fishing vest subcategories (fly-fishing vests, fishing vest life jackets, etc.), we continued to narrow our focus until we could find distinct market gap opportunities.

Although the market gap score for lightweight fishing vests was not significantly higher than the category average, the unusually low number of positive reviews combined with elevated neutral and negative feedback indicated abnormal customer dissatisfaction. This suggested a notable opportunity to address unmet customer needs within this product segment.

Understanding the Market Gap

After identifying a market gap opportunity within lightweight fishing vests, it was time to investigate the specifics of the market gap language. Using the engine’s natural language processing capabilities, we uncovered the following critical market gaps among 30 ‘lightweight fishing vest’ products analyzed:

Zipper Placement & Functionality

In a vast majority of the product listings, customers mentioned a ‘backwards’ or ‘incorrect’ zipper. This issue arises from fishing vest retailers procuring their products from international manufacturers, where zipper design standards differ from those in the United States.

Zipper Quality

Similarly, customers frequently mentioned poor quality zippers. This issue was highly prominent and closely associated with customers that mentioned backwards zippers.

Inner Pocket Quantity

Customers across product listings consistently mentioned their desire for secure inner zippered pockets. This desire was strongly associated with vest practicality - wanting a reliable pocket to hold their phones and other small important items.

Pocket Size & Accessibility

Lastly, small pocket size, and insufficient pocket access were frequently discussed by customers across the product listings. As we investigated the context behind the reviews that mentioned this issue, it became clear that across a vast majority of products, there were many pockets too small or too awkward to for consistent use.

The Impact

By methodically analyzing fishing equipment products, we identified a prominent product opportunity within the industry: Lightweight fishing vests featuring high-quality zippers that adhere to Western design principles, along with accessible, spacious, inside zippered pockets.

By leveraging its data scraping capabilities and machine learning models, we analyzed thousands of customer reviews and identified key product attributes that consumers actively sought out but didn’t find.
